# KumbhConnect — constraint reality-check (12cr/day · logistics · economics · non-literacy · language)

**Verdict:** KumbhConnect is right about the problem and wrong about where the work is: the cross-center gap is a ~$24k box-plus-Claude problem, and four of the flashiest layers (YOLO, ML-risk, proximity-push, self-serve family search) either run on data that does not exist or actively endanger the people the system serves. Build Setu's safety-gated registry + multilingual matching brain + operator-mediated voice intake; spend the saved $0.6-1.8M on the ~750 multilingual humans who are the actual bottleneck.

## The inversion

The constraints invert the entire value stack. KumbhConnect's pitch front-loads the layers that demo well on a laptop (computer vision, ML risk, a proximity-push volunteer app, a family search bar) and treats offline-first as a mid-tier feature (L4). But on the ground every one of those flashy layers collapses precisely on the peak Amrit Snan day: cellular is down 12-18h, there is no video feed to run YOLO on (verified: CCTV is coordinates-only), the surge that risk-ML would predict is mathematically not in the data (daily counts flat at max/mean=1.40x), the at-risk cohort is phoneless/non-literate/photoless so there is nothing to push, search, or face-match against, and a proximity broadcast of "vulnerable person at Gate X" is a trafficking-lure. What survives is exactly what looks boring: an append-only registry that lives as a full local replica on every box (L4/offline is really L0), a Claude-backed cross-script matching brain that is the ONLY thing that crosses the language barrier, and trained humans doing voice intake. The database never breaks a sweat at 120M/day; the binding constraint is ~750 multilingual operators clearing a 7-min/case queue while partitioned. So the money and the engineering must move from GPUs and ML to humans, offline resilience, and the matching brain — the cheap layers ARE the core, and the expensive layers are the liability.

## The numbers that settle it

- CCTV dataset verified: camera_id,longitude,latitude ONLY — 1,280 CSV rows / 4,141 KML placemarks, ZERO rtsp/stream/video/feed references in either file. L6 YOLO has no physical input.
- Registry + matching: ~$24k one-time (30 boxes x $800) + ~$36/day Claude (haiku, batched/cached) vs YOLO at $576k (1,280 cams/64 GPUs) to $1.8M (4,079 cams/204 GPUs) — a ~25-75x capex gap and ~1000x opex gap for ZERO reunions.
- reporter_mobile is 2008/2008 distinct (100% unique, 0 collisions) — it is the FAMILY's number, dedups reporters not persons, catches zero cross-center person-duplicates. Mobile can never be a person key.
- Surge is NOT learnable: daily case counts are flat — min 40, max 78, mean 55.6, max/mean = 1.40x over 45 days — so an ML risk model would confidently predict flat and miss the one day (4-5x snan) it's needed. The surge must be a static calendar input.
- The cohort that breaks naive search: 14.8% (371) no name, 19.7% (492) no mobile, gender contradicts description ~48%, 60+ is 58% (1454), 288 minors (11.5%) needing a police-only gate. No photo/image/face column exists — face-rec has nothing to match on the missing side.
- Languages are near-uniform across 10 with NO majority: Hindi only 10.8% (271), Marathi — the Nashik operator's own tongue — the SMALLEST at 9.3% (233). 79.8% of records are neither Hindi nor Marathi, so operator<->pilgrim mismatch is the default case.
- Human bottleneck: ~7 min/case x ~96 intakes/operator/day -> ~750 trained multilingual operators across 30+ centers on a ~24,000-case (4,000 concurrent) peak day. Resolution mean 4.0h / max 28.9h confirms the custody tail, not intake, is where backlog grows.

## Honest pushback to the design

- YOLO (L6) is the headline mistake. You cannot run YOLOv11 on a feed that does not exist — I grep-verified both the CSV and KML and there is not one rtsp/stream/video reference, just coordinates. Even with feeds it does crowd-density, NOT person-ID, so it closes zero A-to-B cases. And the crowd-density signal you want is already produced for free by the static risk prior. Cut it; do not keep even a 6-camera version. The biggest economic danger is letting a sponsor anchor on $0.6-1.8M of cameras while the ~750 operators who actually produce reunions go underfunded.
- Face-rec is dead as a query key. There is no photo/image/face column in the data, and structurally the MISSING side is a phoneless elder with ~0% pre-existing photos. 'Search by photo' has nothing to search against for half the people the system exists for. The only honest use is a found-side re-rank, hard-capped at AMBER, enforced in code — never an auto-confirm, never a GREEN-reaching signal alone. Loose photo/demographic confidence is exactly what hands a non-verbal 80-year-old to the wrong 'family'.
- 'Real-time-synced backend' (L1) describes a system that does not exist on the day it matters. Cellular collapses 12-18h on Amrit Snan; a centralized live backend goes blind precisely at peak intake. The architecture must be edge-primary with sneakernet as the floor. Strike those words from the pitch.
- The human-throughput bottleneck is absent from your plan and it is everything. At 120M/day the DB does ~2-3 writes/sec/center — trivial. The system lives or dies on ~750 multilingual operators clearing a 7-min queue, and because the 29h tail is custody-dominated you must staff a SECOND queue (active custody) you never mention. The tempting 'fix' — auto-suggesting demographic matches to clear the queue — is the catastrophic failure the band-governor exists to prevent.
- L9's proximity-push is not just useless, it's dangerous. A 400m walk is non-traversable at crush (reachable distance collapses to ~100-150m), it needs the dead network, and 'vulnerable person at Gate X' pushed to a rotating lost/stolen device is a forgeable predator-targeting feed. Keep capture, kill broadcast.
- Every layer that assumes literacy/phones/photos (L10 self-serve search, L2 photo matching, L9 push) breaks on the actual cohort: 14.8% no name, 19.7% no mobile, 58% aged 60+, no photos, 10 languages with the operator's own Marathi the smallest at 9.3%. Family-facing must be operator-mediated voice+icon, full stop.
- L2's deterministic translit fallback silently fails your OWN demo name: Lakshmi vs Lokkhi (Bengali) produce keys lxm vs lk, soundex L250 vs L200 — no collision. The rules encode Hindi-style romanization, not Bengali/Tamil/Telugu sound shifts, so true cross-language pairs are never even generated as candidates. Claude name-equivalence is on the critical path, not optional; pre-store its romanization variants per name at insert.
- L7's XGBoost is precision over noise. The surge is flat in the file (1.40x max/mean) — a learned model predicts flat and misses snan day. Ship the transparent formula and never call it 'AI prediction'; the transparency is the feature.

## Constraint-honest build order

1. L0 Offline-first FIRST: full local replica + append-only log, 12-18h UPS, 40C fanless derating, watchdog restart, and a printed icon+QR paper fallback at every kiosk. Nothing else matters if this fails on snan day.
2. L1 Registry as edge-primary with eventual convergence over a transport ladder ending in sealed-USB sneakernet on a 30-60min courier loop. Cloud is NEVER on the life-safety path.
3. The matching brain (L2+L3 fused): deterministic phonetic/translit blocking as recall insurance + Claude-haiku cross-script name-equivalence and semantic description match as the precision layer. Gender out of the key; GREEN requires a strong identifier; demographics-only hard-capped at AMBER.
4. Operator-mediated multilingual voice intake (L10 reframed): pre-recorded prompts in 10 languages, Claude ASR-cleanup + schema-locked extraction (null stays null), operator read-back by ear, tap-a-landmark + tapped age/gender so a record completes with zero shared language and zero literacy.
5. Staff and budget the ~750 multilingual operators across two queues (intake + custody) — this is the program, not a line item. Pre-position the reserve using the static risk prior.
6. L7 as the transparent static prior (geo_intel.py): chokepoint_risk x snan_multiplier x (1+report_density), output as 'pre-staff these 6 chokepoints on 2027-09-11'. No XGBoost.
7. Capture-only roving tablets (L9 reframed) + lean cloud-secondary supervisor dashboard (L8). Defer the full geo operational map (L5) to Phase 4 after the GPS gazetteer walk.
8. CUT L6 YOLO entirely; redirect the $0.6-1.8M to operators.

## What to build & demo

Demo the partition, not the dashboard. Show two physically separated boxes (Center A, Center B) with the network cable pulled. (1) At A, an operator does a voice intake of a found, confused, non-verbal elder in a non-Marathi dialect — pre-recorded prompt, Claude ASR-cleanup + schema-locked extraction into desc_struct, operator reads parsed fields back by ear, location entered by tapping a landmark, photo captured at the found-moment, no name typed. The record gets a ULID and a phonetic/translit key on-box in under 2s, fully offline. (2) At B (still partitioned), the searching family — non-literate, phoneless — has an operator search by spoken cross-language description; show the deterministic blocker casting wide and Claude resolving Lokkhi==Lakshmi where the fallback alone fails, returning a confirm-only projection (case_id/band/age_band/zone/thumbnail, NEVER names). (3) Reconnect via a USB 'courier' and show eventual convergence + the FOUND<->MISSING bridge surfacing, gated to AMBER 'visual check required' because it's demographics+description (no strong identifier), with identity-proof interrogating the searching family. (4) Show the negative-control: a literal stranger with matching demographics stays AMBER and is NEVER auto-suggested as GREEN. (5) Run geo_intel.py to print 'pre-position the reserve at these 6 very-high-risk chokepoints on 2027-09-11' from the static snan x chokepoint x density prior. Close on cost: this entire reunification capability is ~$24k + ~$36/day, versus the $0.6-1.8M YOLO farm that has no feed and reunites no one.

## Economics

The cross-center gap is closed by ~$24-27k of one-time, multi-mela-reusable hardware (30 rugged $800 boxes + spares, tablets, UPS, radios) plus ~$36/day of batched, prompt-cached Claude-haiku — hard-capped at ~$150-200/day even on a 5x snan day, totaling ~$2-4k over a 45-day mela. That is the cheapest line in the entire system and the only one that produces reunions, costing well under $0.20/case in software. Every other big number is either a trap or the real program: the YOLO build ($576k-$1.8M) buys zero reunions on a feed that does not exist and must be zeroed out of every phase; the ML risk engine is un-trainable on flat data and reduces to a free CPU lookup. The actual recurring cost — and the binding constraint — is the human payroll: ~750 trained multilingual operators on a peak day across two queues (intake + the custody-dominated 29h tail), plus stipends, a gazetteer GPS re-walk, and SIM/data. The single decision that makes the program affordable AND effective is the same one: take the $0.6-1.8M you were going to spend on cameras and spend it on humans, because humans are what every reunion is actually made of.

## Closing judgment

KumbhConnect diagnosed the right disease — a found person at Center A is invisible to a family at Center B — and then prescribed mostly the wrong medicine. The cure is small, cheap, and unglamorous: an offline-first append-only registry, a Claude-backed cross-script matching brain that is the only thing capable of crossing 10 languages and 5 romanizations per name, operator-mediated voice intake for a phoneless non-literate cohort, and ~750 multilingual humans who are the true throughput ceiling. That is Setu, and it is CORE. The disease is the four flashy layers — YOLO, ML-risk, proximity-push, self-serve family search — which are not merely wasteful: under the snan-day partition they are a false sense of capability that evaporates exactly when needed, and two of them (proximity broadcast, loose photo/demographic auto-confirm) are active safety hazards that can hand a vulnerable elder to a predator. Cut L6 entirely, reduce L7 to a transparent prior, invert L9 to capture-only, and recast L10 as operator-mediated. Then take the $0.6-1.8M you saved and put it where every reunion is actually produced: trained multilingual humans, rugged cheap boxes, and the matching brain. Build the partition demo, not the dashboard demo — that is the difference between a hackathon slide and a system that finds a lost grandmother on the one day 120 million people show up.
